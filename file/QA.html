Question:How do you deal with ransomware?

anonymous4

Answer:CryptoLocker is normally delivered as a ‘payload’ file attached to an email or can be download from a malicious website or via a botnet command and control server if your computer has previously been infected and not properly cleaned up. We have received to date less than a dozen reports of CryptoLocker infections in New Zealand but in other parts of the world authorities have reported hundreds of users per day finding their files are encrypted. The most common route of infection is where a computer user opens a malicious file attached to an email, normally an Adobe PDF that begins the file encryption process. Common email subjects have covered payroll or online banking alerts, parcel delivery dockets and other subjects that might encourage the recipient to open the email and then the attached file. Note: If your computer is connected to a USB drive, local backup device or a network storage system it’s also possible for CryptoLocker to jump onto these systems and encrypt files there too.

Question:What is a man-in-the-middle attack, and how can you prevent it?

anonymous8

Answer:Man in the Middle (MITM) attack is a kind of cyber attack where an attacker steals the communication channel between two parties. Through MITM an attacker can do the following 01) Steal the login credentials 02) Alter the communication between two parties and take the overall control of their conversation. 03) Session Hijacking 04) Steal Account Details / Personal Data and many more. The MiTM attack may take place in multple ways. 01) Free Wifi 02) ARP Spoofing 03) mDNS Spoofing 04) DNS Spoofing To prevent MITM, follow these steps 01) Implementation of HSTS (HTTP Strict Transport Security) will be suggestable. 02) Never get connected to Free Wifi Zones / Open hot spots. 03) Logout the applications properly. Never close the applications. 04) Secure your Wifi connections with strong passwords. 05) Look at the Anti-Virus notifications and browser notifications while connecting websites. 06) Never do bank related transactions in Public free wifi zones / Netcafes.
